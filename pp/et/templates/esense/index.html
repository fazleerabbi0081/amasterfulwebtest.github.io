Translating Europe Forum
 
On the 27th -28th October, Brussels once again hosts the 'Translating Europe Forum' and registration is now open. This annual event is open to anyone involved in the translation industry; from freelance translators to researchers, from Language Service Providers to translators' and translation
employers' associations. This event is free to attend and aims to bring together professionals to evoke dialogue on topics of common interest, look at new technologies and discuss their impact on the future of this field.
 
 
 
Rio 2016: The challenges of the largest multicultural gathering in the world.
 
eSense Translations, like most of the rest of the U.K. enjoyed the great efforts of our athletes in Rio during the Olympics this summer. The organisational task of accommodating the needs of thousands of athletes, with a wide variety of languages and cultures during this event, was undeniably
massive. eSense Translations reviewed just how this was accomplished and what measures were put in place to ensure all the athletes and their support team were given the best chance to perform in front of the world.
 
 
 
The first ever sign language-only TV advertisement
 
Earlier this month, the Guardian reported on Channel 4's broadcast of the first ever sign language-only TV ad. The ad, which was broadcast during the Rio Paralympic Games Opening Ceremony, was initially aired without subtitles, but later broadcasts added them in to allow viewers in on
the joke. We were surprised, on hearing this news, that this is the first advertisement of its kind and think it's great that marketers are now thinking more about appealing to the range of diversities we have in the U.K.
 
 
 
Interpreting in Difficult Situations
 
A theme of a range of our blogs this month has been interpreting in different and sometimes difficult scenarios. Whilst compiling this information, it really highlighted how crucial interpreters' roles are and how much they assist with important decisions in people's lives. From interpreters working in a conflict zone to those who assist patients at the end of the lives in palliative care, the value they provide is undeniable.
 
 
 
 
The Untranslatable!
 
I'd like to conclude our newsletter this month on a lighter note. Take a look at these phrases that amused our team and which do not have an equivalent translation in English.
 
How many of these have you experienced?!
 
Tartle (Scottish): This is the awkward moment when you want to introduce someone, but hesitate because you have forgotten their name!
 
Jayus (Indonesian): This word describes a joke that is so poorly told that you cannot help but laugh!
 
Forelsket (Norwegian): The English have tried to translate this, but it has resulted in a rather clumsy phrase of New Relationship Energy.' It is that glorious feeling of happiness and excitement that you experience when you are falling in love. It is the adrenaline, the butterflies and nerves that accompany the novelty of spending time with a person and knowing that it is special.
 
For more of these untranslatable expressions, check out our blog, 'Words can't express this sentiment.'
